# Living Lands Reloaded

**When the world feels full, but your character feels the same on day 30, survival needs a soul.**

One more ore run turns into a dry sprint home, one bar from collapse.

You don't make it back because you babysat a meter.
You make it back because you played your character.

**Living Lands Reloaded makes those identities matter.**

This mod turns Hytale into an **RPG survival experience** where your needs create stakes, and your professions create a long-term character arc. Journeys require preparation, combat has a cost, and your choices shape who you become.

---

## üß≠ RPG Survival: Needs That Create Stakes

You get three clear HUD bars on join (simple to read, hard to ignore):

- **Hunger** - Your fuel for the day
- **Thirst** - Your endurance for travel
- **Energy** - Your ability to push hard (sprinting, fighting, extended work)

Baseline drain is paced for normal gameplay, but effort matters:

- Sprinting ramps drain up fast
- Combat increases depletion across the board
- Long swims and harsh travel punish unprepared players

When a stat drops into the danger zone, you don't just "see a number". You feel it in your moment-to-moment play:

- **Starvation** - weakness and damage pressure
- **Dehydration** - movement and stamina pressure
- **Exhaustion** - sprint and combat become desperate

Different foods restore different needs. Your inventory becomes a kit, not a pile.

## üé≠ Choices That Define Your Character

Living Lands isn't about babysitting meters. It's about turning routine play into **role-driven decisions**:

- You're hurt and being chased: sprint now and crash later, or slow down and risk the hit?
- You're mid-build and "almost done": keep placing blocks or break for food before the penalties start?
- You're staring at a distant dungeon: commit with what you packed, or turn back and do it properly?

Survival becomes the pressure. Your build, habits, and profession become the answer.

## üèÖ Professions: Your Character Sheet In Motion

Survival provides the stakes. **Professions provide the fantasy.**

Every action contributes to growth, so progression feels natural instead of grindy:

- Break ore: Mining XP
- Chop trees: Logging XP
- Fight enemies: Combat XP
- Place blocks: Building XP
- Pick up resources: Gathering XP

**Five professions, 100 levels each**, with meaningful passive milestones at **15 / 45 / 100**.

Highlights (examples):

- **Mining** - progression boosts, thirst capacity perks, and bonus ore potential
- **Logging** - energy perks and extra log drops at mastery
- **Combat** - hunger capacity perks and burst mobility after kills
- **Building** - stamina perks and a chance to conserve materials at the top tier
- **Gathering** - pickup bonuses and long-term depletion reduction for true survivors

**Death has consequences:** dying costs **85% XP in 2 random professions**. It turns reckless play into a real gamble, and it makes "we made it back" feel like an accomplishment.

## üõ†Ô∏è Built For Server Owners (Not Just Players)

Living Lands Reloaded is designed to be fun for players and painless for admins, with enough control to fit anything from casual RP to hardcore survival.

- **Drop-in setup** - install the jar, boot the server, configs generate automatically
- **Hot reload** - tune values without restarts (`/ll reload`)
- **Per-world rules** - different RPG/survival profiles per world (adventure, arena, creative, hardcore)
- **Fully configurable** - every number can be adjusted to fit your server's culture
- **Smart defaults** - balanced out of the box, but easy to push toward hardcore

Per-world examples:

- Creative world: disable metabolism
- Adventure world: default tuned for exploration and pacing
- Hardcore world: faster drain and harsher penalties
- PvP arena: combat pushes energy harder
- Heat-themed world: thirst becomes the primary threat

Same mod, multiple experiences.

## üì¶ Installation

Players: join a server running Living Lands Reloaded. The HUD appears automatically.

Server owners:

1. Download `livinglands-reloaded-1.0.1.jar`
2. Place it in your server `plugins/` folder
3. Start the server
4. Configs generate in `LivingLandsReloaded/config/`
